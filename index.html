Common Garage Door Issues and How Professionals Fix Them

Let’s take a look at some common garage door issues and how professional technicians can fix them.

1. Broken Springs

Garage door springs are under a lot of tension, and over time, they can break. This is one of the most common issues and can prevent your garage door from opening or closing properly. Professional technicians can safely replace the broken springs, restoring your garage door to full functionality.

2. Misaligned Tracks

If your garage door is off-track, it can cause the door to operate unevenly or not at all. Technicians can realign the tracks, ensuring that the door moves smoothly and without any hiccups.

3. Malfunctioning Openers

A garage door opener that’s not working can be frustrating. Whether it’s a problem with the remote, the motor, or the sensors, professionals can diagnose and fix the issue, getting your garage door back in action.

4. Worn-Out Rollers

Rollers can wear out over time, causing the door to operate noisily or unevenly. Replacing worn-out rollers is a quick and easy fix for professionals, and it can make a world of difference in how your garage door operates.

5. Damaged Panels

If your garage door has been damaged (maybe from an overenthusiastic game of backyard cricket), professionals can replace the damaged panels, restoring the door’s appearance and functionality.

Tips for Maintaining Your Garage Door

While professional repairs are essential, there are also some things you can do to keep your garage door in good shape between service calls.

1. Regular Inspections

Take a few minutes every month to inspect your garage door. Look for any signs of wear and tear, such as frayed cables, loose bolts, or misaligned tracks. Catching issues early can prevent bigger problems down the line.

2. Keep It Clean

Dirt and debris can build up on your garage door and its components, causing it to operate less efficiently. Regularly clean the door, tracks, and rollers to keep everything running smoothly.

3. Lubricate Moving Parts

Lubricating the moving parts of your garage door, such as the rollers, hinges, and springs, can reduce friction and prevent wear and tear. Just be sure to use a lubricant that’s specifically designed for garage doors.

4. Test the Balance

An unbalanced garage door can put extra strain on the opener and other components. To test the balance, disconnect the opener and manually lift the door. If it doesn’t stay in place when partially open, it may need to be rebalanced by a professional.

5. Listen for Unusual Noises

If your garage door starts making strange noises, don’t ignore it. Unusual sounds can be a sign that something is wrong, and addressing the issue early can prevent more serious problems.
